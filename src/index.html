Earthquake Information

Earthquakes are caused by the sudden release of energy in the Earth's crust, creating seismic waves that shake the ground. They can vary in magnitude, depth, and location. Learn more about earthquakes.

Earthquake Hazards

Earthquakes can cause various hazards, including ground shaking, landslides, tsunamis, and structural damage to buildings. It's important to be prepared and know what to do during an earthquake.

Magnitude

Magnitude measures the energy released by an earthquake, with higher magnitudes indicating stronger earthquakes. Learn more about earthquake magnitude.

Hypocentral Depth

Hypocentral depth refers to the depth at which an earthquake occurs beneath the Earth's surface. It is an essential factor in understanding the earthquake's impact on the surface and the potential for damage. Learn more about hypocentral depth.

⚠️ Warning: Earthquake Detected Near Your Location

An earthquake with significant magnitude has been detected near your area. Please take necessary precautions and stay safe.

During earthquakes, drop to the floor, take cover under a desk or table, and hold on to it so that it doesn't move a way from you. Wait there until the shaking stops
